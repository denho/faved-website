Switching to Faved doesn't mean leaving your existing bookmarks behind. You can import directly from
your browser, from Raindrop.io, and from Pocket, keeping your tags and folder structure intact.

> [!TIP]
> After any import, [bulk actions](/docs/guides/bulk-actions) make it easy to reorganize tags or refetch
> metadata across all the newly imported bookmarks at once.

## Import from your browser

You can import bookmarks directly from your browser in just a few clicks. **Chrome, Firefox, Safari,
Edge, and most other browsers are supported.**

The import fully preserves your browser's bookmark **folder structure by converting folders into tags**,
which you can then reorganize however you prefer using nested tags.

<Steps>

    <Step title="Export your bookmarks from your browser">

        Most browsers let you export bookmarks as an HTML file from their bookmark manager.

    </Step>

    <Step title="Open the browser import page in Faved">

        Navigate to **Settings → Import → From Browser**

    </Step>

    <Step title="Choose your file and start the import">

        Select your exported HTML file and start the import.

        The import usually takes a few seconds, and your folder
        structure will be converted into hierarchical tags automatically.

        Once complete, you'll see a confirmation message displaying the
        total number of bookmarks that were imported.

    </Step>

</Steps>

## Import from Raindrop.io

If you're switching from Raindrop.io, you can import your bookmarks **along with their tags**, so your
existing structure stays intact and ready to use right away.

<Steps>

    <Step title="Export your collection from Raindrop.io">

        In Raindrop.io, use the export option to download your collection.

    </Step>

    <Step title="Open the Raindrop.io import page in Faved">

        Navigate to **Settings → Import → From Raindrop.io**

    </Step>

    <Step title="Select your export file and start the import">

        Choose your export file and start the import.

        The import usually takes a few seconds, and your collection will be converted into hierarchical tags
        automatically. Raindrop.io tags will also be preserved.

        Once complete, you'll see a confirmation message displaying the
        total number of bookmarks that were imported.

    </Step>

</Steps>

## Import from Pocket

Faved imports a Pocket export, bringing across your saved and archived articles, tags, collections, and
notes.

<Steps>

    <Step title="Export your data from Pocket">

        > [!TIP]
        > Pocket sometimes omits article excerpts and titles for articles that aren't attached to a collection.
        > If you can, add your saved articles to a collection before exporting to improve the quantity and
        > quality of the data Pocket includes.

        1. Go to the Pocket export page at https://getpocket.com/export and sign in if needed.
        2. Click **Export CSV file** to start the export. Pocket prepares the file and emails you a download
        link when it's ready.
        3. Open the email titled **Update on your Pocket data export**, follow the link, and download your
        export. It's a ZIP file containing your saved and archived links, tags, collections, and notes.

    </Step>

    <Step title="Open the Pocket import page in Faved">

        Navigate to **Settings → Import → From Pocket**

    </Step>

    <Step title="Select your export file and start the import">

        Choose the ZIP file you downloaded from Pocket and start the import. You don't need to unzip it, Faved
        handles that for you.

        The import usually takes just a few seconds depending on the size of your export.
        Once complete, you'll see a confirmation message displaying the
        total number of bookmarks that were imported.

    </Step>

</Steps>

Here's what you'll get after the import:

- All imported bookmarks receive an **Imported from Pocket** tag.
- Unread and Archived bookmarks are assigned corresponding tags under a **Status** parent tag.
- Collections are imported as tags under a **Collections** parent tag.
- Collection descriptions are preserved as tag descriptions.
- Bookmark notes are saved as bookmark comments.

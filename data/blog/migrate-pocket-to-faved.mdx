As you probably already know, Pocket is [shutting down](https://getpocket.com/farewell) its service on **July 8, 2025**.
This means that you will no longer be able to access your articles and other content saved in Pocket after that date: the app and browser extension will stop working, and website will transition into export-only mode.
You will be able to export your data until **October 8, 2025**.

Faved is a great alternative to Pocket. Not only it lets you seamlessly organize your links, articles, and other web content - it also gives you full control and ownership of your data.
Faved database is stored locally on your computer or server, and is never sent to any third-party servers.

If you want to migrate your data from Pocket to Faved, you can do it in a few simple steps.

3. Check your inbox for email with subject **Update on your Pocket data export**. Click the provided link and download your export file. This will be a ZIP file containing all your saved and archived article links, tags, collections, and notes.

## ðŸ“¥ Step 2: Import Your Data to Faved

1. Open Faved in browser and go to the **Import from Pocket** page. ![Import from Pocket button](/static/images/posts/migrate-pocket-to-faved/import-from-pocket-button.png)
2. Click **Choose file** and select the ZIP file you downloaded from Pocket. You don't need to unzip it, Faved will do it for you.![Import from Pocket screen](/static/images/posts/migrate-pocket-to-faved/import-from-pocket-screen.png)
3. Click the **Import Bookmarks** button to start the import process. Faved will read the ZIP file and extract the data from it. This will take just a few seconds depending on the size of your export file.
4. Once the import is complete, you will see a success message with the number of bookmarks imported. ![Import from Pocket success message](/static/images/posts/migrate-pocket-to-faved/import-from-pocket-success.png)

- Collections will be imported as tags under **Collections** parent tag.

- Collection bookmark notes will be saved as bookmark comments.

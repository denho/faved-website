Managing your library one bookmark at a time is now a thing of the past. Select as many bookmarks as you like and act on them all at once — delete them in bulk, add or update tags across the whole selection, or refetch metadata for every item in a single operation. It's a much faster way to tidy up after a big import, reorganize your tags, or clean out links you no longer need, saving you from repeating the same action dozens of times.

## Metadata refetching in list view

You no longer need to open each bookmark individually to refresh its details. Right from the list view, you can refetch a bookmark's metadata — its title, description, and preview image — in a single click. This is especially handy when a page has been updated since you saved it, or when a bookmark was imported without complete information, letting you keep your library accurate and up to date without breaking your flow.

## Cleaner and more intuitive pagination controls

We've also given pagination a fresh, simpler look. Moving between pages is now clearer and more predictable, with a tidier layout that shows exactly where you are in your library and makes it easy to jump to the page you want. It's a small change, but it makes browsing large collections of bookmarks feel noticeably smoother.
